The Sport

India's Ancient
Game, Reimagined

Lagori — also known as Pittu Garam or Seven Stones — is a traditional Indian outdoor sport played for over 5,000 years. Two teams compete: one tries to knock down a stack of flat stones with a rubber ball, while the other attempts to rebuild the stack before being hit.

Associated with Lord Shree Krishna himself, Lagori is referenced in ancient texts including ShreeKrishna Mahatmya and the Mahabharata. The Washim Lagori Association is proudly affiliated with the Amateur Lagori Federation of India (ALFI) — the national body driving this heritage sport back into the spotlight.

Global Heritage

Recognized Across
the World

Lagori's roots stretch across continents — documented in history books, census records, and academic journals worldwide.

🇮🇳
India — 1961 Census
Lagori is officially mentioned in the 1961 Census of India (Vol. 11, Part 6, Issue 37), cementing its place in India's cultural record.
📚
American Library Association
Featured in the ALA's acclaimed book "Venture into Cultures" — acknowledging Lagori as a globally significant traditional game.
🕌
Iran — Haftsang
Known as Haftsang in Iran, the game appears in Iranian Art & Culture and is supported by academic research from Farhangian University (2012).
🇦🇲
Armenia — Philately Stamps
Armenia commemorated Lagori with official Children's Philately Stamps, recognising it as a shared part of global childhood heritage.
✨
Lord Krishna's Game
Referenced in ShreeKrishna Mahatmya (chapters 272–317) and Shrikrishna Charitra — Lagori was played by Lord Krishna and is linked to the Mahabharata era.
🌍
Taylor & Francis — 2008
Recognised in "The City of South Asia" (Taylor & Francis, 2008) as a culturally significant sport woven into the fabric of South Asian life.
Source: indialagori.in — Amateur Lagori Federation of India (ALFI)
